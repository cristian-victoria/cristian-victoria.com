Observer and Participant of life

Everything in this world, whether tangible or intangible, is one piece of a puzzle that is life. In Hindu cultures, it is said that we are all made up of the same thing; just as clay can have different forms, our bodies and our lives are a product of the same thing that plants, other animals, and inanimate objects are made of. In that case, we are all participants of life.

Student of the World

In an age marked by advancing technology and artificial intelligence, in the pursuit for ultimate comfort and convenince we can forget our duty to uphold our core values of curiosity. There is so much to the world yet to be explored, learned about, and appreciated, and yet we conform to the societal pressure of letting these technologies take control of a lot of our day to day actions that we lose track of what it is to be curious.

Seeker of spiritual truth

My spiritual journey is one that will last me a lifetime. I do not think that I will ever be able to obtain the answer to the existencial question that persists in this word - why are we all here, now, alive and present at this exact moment in time? But this will not stop me, or neither should it stop anyone from embarking on their own spiritual journeys. If we as a conscious species are to have purpose, or a responsibility in other words, it is this. This is our ultimate contribution to the world.
